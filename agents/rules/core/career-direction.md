# Career direction

## Purpose

This file prevents campaign drift. Agents must use it before recommending roles,
companies, public positioning, or profile rewrites.

## Primary identity

Dídac is not positioned as a generic junior backend developer, data-quality
operator, or AI-content creator.

The target identity is:

> Systems-minded software engineer building model-agnostic agentic workflows,
> formal technical documentation, and creative engineering artifacts with
> regulated-domain maturity.

## Role lanes

Use the S-001 positioning report as the source model, with the correction below:

1. **Agentic systems / AI workflow engineering** — main differentiator and public
   proof engine.
2. **Research engineering / applied AI** — strong fit when the role rewards
   investigation, formal documentation, tooling, ML/AI study, or creative AI.
3. **ML/data systems with domain depth** — acceptable only when it means
   engineering systems, pipelines, tooling, automation, or model-adjacent work.
4. **Backend/platform** — fallback only at product companies, and only when the
   role avoids the CRUD consulting trap.

## Explicit non-targets

Do not steer the campaign toward:

- data quality as a primary identity;
- repetitive BI/reporting/data-cleaning roles;
- generic backend consulting;
- body-shop staff augmentation;
- CRUD-only junior backend funnels;
- founder-LARP or fake-startup language.

Data work is acceptable only when it is creative, systems-oriented, automated,
model-adjacent, or connected to research and engineering judgment.

## Company filter

Prefer companies and teams with at least one of these traits:

- product ownership rather than outsourced ticket delivery;
- research, R&D, AI, tooling, infrastructure, or developer-product depth;
- regulated-domain complexity where banking or insurance experience helps;
- visible engineering culture, documentation culture, or open-source literacy;
- Barcelona/Catalonia relevance with hybrid or local-network value.

Avoid or deprioritize companies and roles with these signals:

- staff-augmentation language;
- unpaid internship pressure;
- offers below the documented exploitation threshold;
- relocation outside Barcelona as a requirement;
- equity-only or pre-seed instability;
- roles sold as AI but implemented as dashboard maintenance or manual cleanup.

## Campaign rule

The campaign must lead with inspectable proof. The repo, reports, commits,
architecture, workflows, and public artifacts are the evidence. Social content is
only distribution.

Agentic engineering is not decoration. It is the differentiator.
