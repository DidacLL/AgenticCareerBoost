## 4. Hybrid/Remote Baseline 2026

### Current Distribution (Barcelona Tech Sector)

| Work Model | Estimated % | Trend (2024→2026) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hybrid (2–3 days office)** | ~60–65% | ↑ Consolidating as default | [S25][S26] |
| **Fully on-site** | ~20–25% | ↑ Slight increase (return-to-office wave) | [S26][S27] |

### Key Data Points

- **70% of 22@ professionals** prefer hybrid work arrangements [S25]
- **91% of surveyed professionals** prioritize workspace flexibility [S25]
- **22@ office take-up** increased 22% in 2024 (280,000 m² contracted), signaling employer commitment to physical space [S25]
- Of 179 DevOps postings on LinkedIn BCN, **41 are fully remote** (~23%) [S09]
- Barcelona job offerings are **97% full-time**, with hybrid being the dominant arrangement [S02]

### Trend Analysis

The remote work high-water mark was 2021–2022. Since then, Barcelona has followed the global return-to-hybrid pattern. Key dynamics:

1. **International scale-ups** (N26, Revolut, etc.) tend to offer more flexible remote policies due to distributed-by-default culture
2. **Local product companies** (Typeform, Wallapop, Factorial) have settled on hybrid (2–3 office days)
3. **Research institutions** (BSC, i2CAT) typically require on-site presence for lab/infrastructure work, with some flexibility
4. **Consulting/outsourcing** firms often mandate client-site presence
5. **Gaming studios** (King) tend to be on-site heavy due to collaboration requirements [S18]

**For Dídac**: Hybrid is the realistic baseline. Fully remote junior positions exist but are rare and highly competitive. On-site willingness increases employability, especially for first role. `[Inference]`

## Appendix: Methodological Notes

### What this report is and is not

This report synthesizes publicly available data from job boards, salary surveys, recruitment firm reports, company career pages, and press coverage. It does **not** contain insider information or proprietary datasets.

### Known biases in sources

- **Salary aggregators** (Glassdoor, PayScale, Levels.fyi) skew toward international/English-speaking companies, which pay above the Spanish median. Local companies posting on InfoJobs or LinkedIn in Spanish often pay 15–25% less.
- **Some sources** (engineersalarydata.com, hireflow.net) report figures in USD or use inflated ranges that do not match Spanish market reality. Where these conflict with Manfred, Michael Page, or Glassdoor data anchored to Spain/Barcelona, the latter are preferred.
- **"Junior ML Engineer €42K"** reports likely sample from international companies (HP, Amazon, etc.) rather than the broad market. A realistic floor for a career-changer's first ML role at a local company is closer to €24K–€28K. `[Inference]`

### Career-changer realism check

Entry-level job postings in Spain fell 34% vs. 2020 [S29]. Bootcamp output continues to flood the generalist junior market [S28]. Dídac's competitive advantages are:

1. **UOC CS degree with ML/AI mention** — qualifies for positions that reject bootcamp-only candidates
2. **15 years professional maturity** — valuable to employers tired of flight-risk junior hires
3. **English proficiency** — commands a 15–20% salary premium at international companies [S05]
4. **Portfolio differentiation** — this agentic system project demonstrates systems thinking, AI engineering, and documentation discipline beyond what most juniors can show `[Inference]`

His competitive disadvantages are:

1. **Age perception** — Spain has documented age bias; 36 is not critical (threshold is ~45) but may trigger unconscious filtering at some companies [S15]

**Net assessment**: Dídac's profile is stronger than the median career changer entering Barcelona's tech market, primarily due to the formal CS degree with ML specialization.
