## 3. Structural Patterns

### 3.1 Hook Patterns That Work for Dídac's Profile

Based on analysis of S1, S2, S3, and all corpus entries:

| Pattern | Template | Example for Dídac | Expected engagement |
|---------|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Age + pivot + specificity** | "At [age], I [did something unexpected]. [Specific detail]." | "At 36, I built a 6-agent multiagent system to get my first tech job. The system itself became the portfolio." | High (3.7-4.2% based on S1 data) |
| **Contradiction** | "I [did counterintuitive thing]. On purpose." | "I open-sourced my entire job search strategy. On purpose." | High (triggers curiosity + disagreement) |
| **Architecture reveal** | "Here's the actual [document] for [real system]." | "Here's the actual agent routing file for a model-agnostic multiagent career system." | Very high (S4: document uploads 5-10x) |
| **Before/after numbers** | "[Metric] before → [metric] after. [One-line context]." | "0 public engineering artifacts → 12 documented sprint outputs in 6 weeks. Built by agents I designed." | High (3.9% per S1) |
| **Honest admission** | "I [confession]. Here's what happened." | "I have 15 years of experience — none of it in tech. Here's how I'm engineering around that." | Very high (failure/admission stories: 4.8% per S1) |

### 3.2 Post Body Structure

Optimal structure derived from S1, S2, S3:

```
[Hook line — no emoji, max 140 chars]
                                          ← blank line
[1-2 sentence context: who you are, why this matters]
                                          ← blank line
[3-4 short paragraphs: the meat]
[One thought per line, max 2-3 lines per paragraph]
[Include at least one specific number, tool name, or commit reference]
                                          ← blank line
[Key takeaway or lesson — formatted as arrow points if multiple]
→ Point one (specific and actionable)
→ Point two (specific and actionable)
                                          ← blank line
[CTA: one specific question inviting experience-sharing]
                                          ← blank line
#relevanthashtag #another #third
```

### 3.3 Length Distribution

| Length | Engagement rate (S1) | Use case for Dídac |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| < 500 chars | 1.2% | Avoid. Too short for technical content. |
| 500-1,000 chars | 2.1% | Quick "shipped this today" updates. |
| **1,000-1,500 chars** | **3.8% (sweet spot)** | **Primary format. Career narrative + one technical proof point.** |
| 1,500-2,000 chars | 3.2% | Deeper architecture posts with diagrams as companion images. |
| > 2,000 chars | 1.5-2.4% | Reserve for LinkedIn articles/newsletters, not feed posts. |

## 5. Tricks and Advice Catalog

### 5.1 First-Post Strategy

| Advice | Source | Specifics |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Lead with the system, not the person** | Inference from A3, A4, A6 patterns | Your first post should not be "Hi, I'm Dídac and I'm looking for a job." It should be "I built a multiagent system that [does X]. Here's the architecture." The person becomes interesting *because* the system is interesting. |
| **Post on Tuesday or Wednesday, 7-9 AM CET** | S1 (data-backed) | Tuesday has 3.2% average engagement. Morning posting catches the commute-scroll window. For Barcelona time, this means posting ~7-8 AM CET to hit both local and UK/Central European feeds. |
| **Respond to every comment within the first hour** | S1: "Posts where the author replied within the first hour got 67% more total engagement." | The first 60-90 minutes are critical (S4: determines 70% of total reach). Block this time. |
| **Put the link in the first comment, never the post body** | S4: external links cause 60% reach penalty. | Write the story in the post. Add "Full repo and documentation: [link]" as the first comment immediately after posting. |

## 8. Key Takeaways for Sprint S-000

1. **The first post should fuse career-change narrative with technical proof.** "I have 15 years of experience — none in tech. So I built a multiagent system." This combines the two highest-engagement patterns.

2. **The repo is the evidence.** Every LinkedIn claim must link back to a commit, a file, or a diagram in the public repository. Brand.md's "evidence over adjectives" is also the algorithm's preference in 2026 (Depth Score rewards genuine depth).

3. **Native document uploads are the format edge.** The 2026 algorithm heavily favors on-platform PDFs. Dídac's engineering documentation (AGENTS.md, truth hierarchy, sprint contracts) is ready-made content for this format.

6. **Barcelona networking amplifies LinkedIn.** BCN Engineering, GDG Barcelona, and the Agentic Coding Meetup are the local multiplier. In-person connections → first-hour engagement → algorithmic boost.

7. **Consistency over virality.** One technical post every Tuesday, one artifact/narrative post every Thursday. The 3-month compounding effect (S1: 42K total engagement for consistent posters) matters more than any single viral hit.

8. **The anti-hype positioning is both authentic and strategic.** In a landscape drowning in AI-influencer noise, "I'm an engineer who uses AI as tooling, not an AI evangelist" is a breath of fresh air — and it's the truth.
