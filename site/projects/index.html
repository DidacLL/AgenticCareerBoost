01 / INDEX

How I like to build

I prefer projects that leave a trail: source code, documents, diagrams, reports, and enough notes that another person can understand the shape without asking for a private tour.

Open source principles matter to me because they force a useful kind of honesty. If the work is visible, the structure has to survive real inspection. That is more interesting than a pretty screenshot with no route back to the files.

This page is not ordered by freshness or glamour. It is ordered by what each project explains about how I think: agentic workflows and review gates, rebuildable documents, then older backend work with domain context. Not every artifact is shiny. Good. Shiny is not the same thing as useful.

01B / WHAT TO LOOK FOR

What these projects are supposed to prove

trace

Can the work be followed?

I care about artifacts that explain their own path: source, reports, state files, project pages, and decisions visible enough to argue with.

taste

Did I remove the decorative nonsense?

A project can be visual and still disciplined. The interesting part is knowing what to show, what to hide, and what should never have been generated in the first place.

transfer

Does it connect domains?

Banking operations, backend work, ML/AI studies, LaTeX, and agentic workflows look like different rooms. I am mostly interested in the doors between them.
