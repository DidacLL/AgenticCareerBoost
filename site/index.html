00 / PROFILE

This is my workbench

I build public technical artifacts that can be opened, explained, and questioned: AI workflows, documentation systems, project pages, LaTeX tooling, and small pieces of software that leave a trail. Before engineering, I spent fifteen years around banking and insurance operations. That probably explains my allergy to beautiful systems that cannot answer basic questions like: what changed, why, who checked it, and what breaks if we touch this?

Recruiters can scan the CV and leave. That is fine. This site is for the ones who keep clicking. If someone opens the project pages, I assume they prefer context over slogan, trade-offs over heroic nonsense, and a little human voice over another perfectly polished rectangle pretending work is never messy.

00C / SITE

About this page

This page is static HTML, CSS, and a little JavaScript. I built it with agent help, but the final decisions are mine: what stays, what sounds wrong, what breaks in the browser, and what is too generic to publish.

I am not interested in pretending that a tool has taste. Agents are useful when they make iteration faster; they are dangerous when they make everything sound equally plausible. This site is partly a demonstration of keeping that under control.

The lists behind the site are plain JSON. os-index.json keeps pages and project previews tidy; blog-index.json keeps the writing shelf honest enough to know what is published, drafted, or merely waiting for a real reason to exist.
